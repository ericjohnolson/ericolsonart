"Clay's simplicity, combined with its limitless potential, made it a great source of joy and adventure in my life." - Jim Gion


A community event celebrating the life and work of a renowned local sculptor, [Jim Gion](https://www.wweek.com/arts/2018/07/06/renowned-portland-sculptor-jim-gion-has-died/). Alongside his many public works, Jim sculpted over a thousand portraits of dogs, and we honored his legacy with a dog-focused event. The event consisted of community members and friends coming together to share stories about Jim's life and celebrate his legacy with dog portraits by artist [Roz Crews](http://rozcrews.info/), a clay workshop by artist [Amanda Leigh Evans](https://www.amandaleighevans.com/), a photo booth by [Artist Michael Bernard Stevenson Jr](http://www.michaelstevensonjr.com/), documentaries of Jim's interviews, and an exhibition of Jim's work all hosted by his friend Stefan Kovatchev at [A1 Birdbath](https://portlandgardendecor.com/) in Portland, Oregon.
